Platform foundations

Security and operations that support Kabrios use cases

Evidence and records

Evidence is only useful if access is controlled, records are durable, and actions are attributable.

Workflow and approvals

Assignments, approvals, changes, incidents, and closures should be reviewable and tied to responsible parties.

Leadership reporting

Reporting is stronger when it rolls up from auditable evidence, tracked actions, and clearly scoped controls.

Audit and policy use cases

Auditor access, attestation history, policy exceptions, and control records depend on durable records and role-based access.

Third-party and resilience use cases

Vendor and continuity workflows require scoping, separation of data, traceable tasks, and operational visibility.

Continuous monitoring

Monitoring must be backed by logging, visibility into events, and disciplined operational review rather than static attestations alone.

How Kabrios approaches trust

Principles that support the operating model

  • Security-relevant actions should be attributable and reviewable
  • Customer data and access should remain clearly separated by tenant and role
  • Operational visibility should support investigation, oversight, and reporting
  • Least privilege should separate administrative actions from standard user operations
  • Trust should be explained in clear language that maps back to the work customers care about
